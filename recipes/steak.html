Steak and Vegetables

Description

This one-pan steak and vegetable dinner is a weeknight winner—juicy sirloin steak seared to perfection alongside crisp-tender broccoli, baby potatoes, and bell peppers, all roasted with garlic and herbs for a rich, savory finish. The charred edges of the steak contrast beautifully with the caramelized vegetables, creating a balanced, nutrient-packed plate that’s as satisfying as it is simple. With minimal cleanup and maximum flavor, it’s a go-to for anyone craving comfort food with a fresh twist.

Ingredients

  • 450 g sirloin steak
  • 450 g baby potatoes
  • 450 g broccoli florets
  • 1 yellow capsicum (approx. 150 g)
  • 1 medium red onion (approx. 150g)
  • 5 garlic cloves, minced
  • 60 ml olive oil
  • 5 g italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to high fan bake
  2. Boil potatoes in salted water for 10-12 minutes until fork-tender; drain and set aside.
  3. Arrange steak and vegetables on a large baking tray in a single layer.
  4. Mix seasoning: Combine minced garlic, olive oil, Italian herbs, salt, and pepper in a small bowl.
  5. Bake for 4-5 minutes per side, flipping steak once, until browned and veggies are slightly charred.
  6. Rest steak for 5 minutes before slicing to retain juices.
  7. Serve immediately, optionally garnished with fresh herbs or a squeeze of lemon.
